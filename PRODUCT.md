# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The core audience is casual manga and manhwa readers aged 16 to 35, primarily on mobile. They read during short breaks, commutes, evenings, and moments of downtime. They often discover new series through TikTok, YouTube, Reddit, Discord, and other fandom spaces, so they expect quick access, low friction, and a strong sense of what is trending now.

The secondary audience is power readers with larger libraries. They track many ongoing series, read across devices, value bookmarks and history, and benefit from organization, progress tracking, notifications, and collections. These features should support daily return behavior without overwhelming the casual reader.

## Product Purpose

Yomi is a manga discovery and reading destination. Most readers already understand how to open and read a chapter; the product's larger job is to make discovery feel memorable enough that users want to return every day.

The first impression should communicate a premium anime-inspired identity, fast discovery of new content, clear reasons to continue reading, and a feeling of belonging to a larger world of stories. The desired user reaction is: "One more chapter." The failure state is: "Where do I click next?"

## Brand Personality

Immersive, premium, playful.

Yomi should feel like entering a living anime universe, not browsing a database. The interface should present artwork, recommendations, and reading paths with quality and restraint, while still leaving room for fandom, delight, and personality. It should feel polished and emotionally engaging without becoming childish or noisy.

## Anti-references

Avoid Fandom wiki clutter: banner overload, excessive navigation, weak hierarchy, and competing attention points.

Avoid low-quality manga aggregator patterns: popups, ad-first layouts, inconsistent UI, poor typography, and anything that makes ads feel more important than stories.

Avoid Reddit-style density for the primary experience: information walls, undifferentiated lists, and layouts where every element competes at the same volume.

## Design Principles

Lead with story atmosphere. The homepage should sell discovery and emotional momentum before exposing management mechanics.

Make daily return obvious. Continue reading, trending titles, recommendations, and new updates should be easy to scan and act on quickly.

Keep power features quiet until needed. Library, history, bookmarks, and future collections should strengthen repeat use without dominating the first impression.

Respect the artwork. Covers and manga imagery are the product's strongest visual material; layout, color, and motion should frame them rather than fight them.

Protect the reader's flow. Navigation, reader controls, ads, and account prompts should never make the user wonder where to click next.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Minimum standard is WCAG AA. The product should support full keyboard navigation, visible focus states, screen reader compatibility, reduced-motion preferences, minimum 16px body text, high-contrast reading mode, dark mode and light mode, one-handed mobile use, and automatic reading progress preservation.

Accessibility is a product quality requirement, not a compliance checkbox.
